## Competitive Positioning

### vs. Free tools (The Bag Manager, BBO extension, SOLVER Explorer)
> Free tools are great at one thing. We're great at everything. If you just need exposure numbers during a draft, free extensions work fine. But if you're managing a real portfolio — 20, 50, 100+ rosters — and you want to understand its shape, its strategy balance, and its market position, you need a dedicated analytics platform. That's what we built.

### vs. BBO PRO ($25/mo)
> Similar analytics depth at a lower price, plus features BBO doesn't have (archetype classification, draft flow analysis). We also support DraftKings — BBO is Underdog only.

### vs. Spike Week ($25/mo)
> Spike Week has the broadest platform support (4 platforms) and strong content. But their extension is Firefox-only, and there's no free tier to try before you buy. We offer a free beta period and Chrome support.

### vs. Premium tools (LegUp at $50/mo, Draft Caddy at $30/mo)
> We're not trying to be a simulation engine or a draft optimizer. We're a portfolio mirror — we show you what you have, clearly enough that you know what to do next. If you want AI-driven pick recommendations, those tools exist. If you want to actually understand your portfolio, we're the focused, affordable option.

## Messaging Don'ts

- **Don't name competitors directly** in public copy (landing page, Reddit posts). Use generic framing: "free tools," "expensive subscriptions," "scattered across tabs."
- **Don't claim to be "the best"** — claim to be "the most complete picture at this price."
- **Don't position against the free extension category** — those are complementary, not competitive. Our extension (when built) will coexist with the web app.
- **Don't oversell features we don't have yet** — no live ADP feed, no Chrome overlay, no in-season features. Only market what's built.
