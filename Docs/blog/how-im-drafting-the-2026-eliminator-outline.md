# The two facts that reset the whole draft (short intro)

Keep this tight — the pick-by-pick does the teaching. Just plant the two load-bearing facts:

1. **Week 1 is a 6-of-12 gate.** You draft a 12-person pod; six advance. It behaves like a DFS
   double-up — you only have to beat the median — but it's unrecoverable. A 20th-percentile Week-1
   score eliminates you ~99% of the time.
2. **The money sits at the back.** Reach Week 14 and you lock ~$10K regardless of score, then it
   climbs ($30K → $130K 3rd → $200K 1st).

> ⚠️ Caveat to state once: "Eliminator" is now a *family* of contests. The standard advances 6-of-12
> in Week 1; a tighter cut (e.g., 4-of-12 "Pomeranian") actually wants *more* ceiling. Everything
> below assumes the 6-of-12 double-up. Confirm your specific contest. ADP here is a 6/19 snapshot and
> is noisy this early — that noise is the edge.

**The one-sentence thesis:** in BBM I chase ceiling; in the Eliminator I **protect a floor** and
**back-weight my byes**, because the goal isn't a tournament-winning spike — it's never owning a hole
that hands me an automatic loss in a single week. Watch how every pick below bends to that.

- **Others on the board:** Amon-Ra St. Brown (DET, bye 6 — **gone the very next pick, 9**), Ashton
  Jeanty (LV, bye 13 — pick 10), James Cook (BUF, bye 7 — pick 11), Justin Jefferson (MIN, bye 6 —

- **Eliminator lesson — the payout structure makes a late bye an asset, not a footnote.** Walk the

  higher the EV, because every bye is a zero you might never have to absorb before the money arrives.
  Lamb's Week-14 bye is one I'd *only ever take in this dead week.*
- **The tease (with a receipt):** In a vacuum (and in BBM) I have Amon-Ra slightly over CeeDee. In the
  Eliminator I take CeeDee over Amon-Ra *every single time* — and I mean it literally here: **Amon-Ra
  came off the board the pick right after me, at 9.** The next pick explains why I'm happy I let him go.

- **Others on the board:** Rico Dowdle (RB, bye 9 — pick 81), Jayden Reed (WR, bye 11 — pick 82),
  J.K. Dobbins (RB, bye 10 — pick 83), Blake Corum (RB, bye 11 — pick 84). (Context: the QB run was

- **Why him:** ADP ~80, move **+26** — one of the biggest risers on the entire Eliminator board. A

- **Eliminator lesson — stack *skinny*, not heavy; and take the onesie on value + bye + timing.** Two

  1. **The free stack.** Dak + Lamb is a skinny stack (QB + one pass-catcher) — enough correlation for

     season-long move that makes spike weeks compound; in a single head-to-head week, three teammates
     cannibalize the same touches and drag down your *average*. Too many eggs in one basket is far more
     dangerous here than in BBM.

# Pick 18 (overall 209) — Brandon Aiyuk (WR, SF · bye 8)

- **Others on the board:** Cooper Kupp (WR, bye 11 — pick 210), Rashod Bateman (WR, bye 13 — pick 211),

    least costly place to break it — I'm never thin at WR in Week 8 even with two off.

    basically the price of the value tier: **Christian Kirk, also a 49er on the Week-8 bye, was the
    other option right here at 213.** To buy this caliber of cheap WR upside, I was taking a Week-8 SF
    receiver either way.
  - There's even a silver lining: two 49ers WRs is a *de facto* shallow correlation if that offense pops.
  - The meta-lesson of the whole article: **you build toward a clean rainbow, you don't always finish
    with a perfect one — and knowing *which* imperfection is cheap (a 7th WR's bye) versus fatal (a 2nd
    QB's bye) is the actual skill.**
