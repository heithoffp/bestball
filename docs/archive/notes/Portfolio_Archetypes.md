# The Bimodal Volatility Protocol: Portfolio Thesis

The **Bimodal Volatility Protocol** is an architectural framework designed to capture 0.1% outcomes in large-field fantasy tournaments. It rejects "balanced" drafting in favor of two extreme poles of capital allocation, maximizing mathematical leverage and environmental anti-fragility.

---

## 1. The Core Thesis: Anti-Fragility

Most drafters seek a "safe floor," resulting in mediocre rosters that cannot survive the chaos of an NFL season. This protocol embraces the **Bimodal Distribution**: we concentrate our bets at the "tails" of the risk curve. By avoiding the "mishy-middle" of roster construction, we ensure that when the league experiences high-variance shifts, our portfolio contains the specific structures positioned to dominate.

## 2. The Primary Branches (80% of Portfolio)

To maximize a top-0.1% finish in a 300-roster portfolio, you need to be disciplined. In a field of hundreds of thousands, "getting lucky" isn't enough; you need a roster that is structurally designed to handle the massive scoring requirements of the Week 17 finals.

Here are the strict, "unbreakable" rules for the three archetypes in your portfolio.

---

## 1. Hero RB (45% Allocation)

The goal here is to secure one "Legendary" ceiling at RB while dominating the WR and Flex positions.

* **The Hero Window:** Your "Hero" must be drafted in **Round 1, 2, or 3**. If you haven't taken an RB by the end of Round 3, you have pivoted into a Zero RB build.
* **The "No-Fly" Zone:** After drafting your Hero, you are **forbidden** from drafting another RB until **Round 8 or 9** at the earliest. You must use Rounds 2–7 to hammer WRs, an elite QB, and an elite TE.
* **Final Count:** You should finish with exactly **5 or 6 RBs total**.
* **To your question on "Reaching":** Pulling a high-Round 3 RB into Round 2 is acceptable **only if** they have "1-of-1" legendary upside (think 20+ PPG potential). Do not reach for a "safe" grinder just to fill the slot. If the elite RBs are gone, just switch to Zero RB.

## 2. Zero RB (40% Allocation)

This is a bet on the "Fragility" of the RB position and the "Antifragility" of your own roster.

* **The Entry Point:** You may not draft your first RB until **Round 7** (standard) or **Round 9** (extreme).
* **The Early Capital:** Your first 6–8 picks must be used exclusively on WR, QB, and TE. You should aim for at least 5 WRs in your first 7 picks.
* **The Archetype:** Only draft two types of RBs:
1. **Ambiguous Backfields:** Players in muddled situations where they could become a 20-touch guy via a teammate's injury or performance (e.g., high-end backups).
2. **PPR Scat-backs:** Players who catch 4+ passes a game to provide a "floor" while you wait for your lottery tickets to hit.


* **Final Count:** You must finish with **6 or 7 RBs**. Since you lack an elite "Hero," you need more "bullets" to ensure you find a starter during the playoff weeks.

## 3. Hyper Fragile (15% Allocation)

This is your "Hammer" strategy. You are betting that the RBs you pick are invulnerable and will outscore the elite WRs.

* **The Blitz:** You must draft **3 RBs within the first 5 rounds**. Ideally, your first two picks are RBs (the "Double Anchor").
* **The Hard Cap:** You are **strictly limited to 4 RBs total**. Once you have your four, you never click the RB button again.
* **The WR Overload:** Because you spent so much early capital on RB, your WR room will be "weak." You must compensate with **quantity**. You should finish with **9 or 10 WRs**.
* **The Onesie Strategy:** Since you only have 4 RBs, use the extra roster spots to ensure you have a high-end QB and TE. A 2-QB / 4-RB / 10-WR / 2-TE build is a classic Hyper Fragile "perfect" structure.

In a 300-roster portfolio, **Structure > ADP**. If you are at the 2.11 and your "Hero" target is projected to go at 3.04, **take him**. Losing a few spots of "closing line value" is a minor sin compared to the mortal sin of ending up with a "Balanced" roster that has a 0% chance of winning a $2M prize.
